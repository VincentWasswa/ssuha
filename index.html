Introduction

Settling Refugees in IDP Camps

SSUHA formerly known as Sudan Health Association (SUHA) is a National Non-Governmental Organization (NNGO) currently registered with the South Sudan Relief and Rehabilitation Commission (SSRRC) under the new NGOs bill and ministry of legal affair, founded in 1995 to assist in supporting Sudanese refugees that were in Kenya and those living in the Internally Displaced Peoples (IDP) camps in Bamurye in Kajo-Keji County. The aim was to meet the health and social needs of the refugees and IDPs.

CORE PROGRAMS

Thematic Areas

Health

Coordinate, collaborate and partner with the Ministry of Health (MoH), LGAs and other stakeholders to support health services.

Water & Sanitation

Promote health and hygiene awareness, form and train community-based WASH committees, provide spare parts and tools for rehabilitation of water sources, conduct quality testing to provide safe water, facilitate and promote establishment of water sources, public VIP latrines and household pit latrines.

Sexual & Reproductive Health

Provide ANC and safe motherhood, promote family planning, promote child health, promote adolescent reproductive health, manage abortions, HIV/AIDS/STIs, promote management and prevention of infertility

HIV & AIDS

Promote VCT and access to ARVs, prevent MTCT, promote home-based care, campaign against co-infections, promote the use of condoms, promote community and stakeholders’ engagement in campaign, promote the use of IEC materials and radio talk shows

Food Security & Livelihood

Increase and diversify agricultural production, facilitate surplus production, improve natural resource management and sustainable land use option

Protection

Provide psychosocial support, conduct case management, strengthen community linkages and referral pathways for SGBV, provide women and girls/boys friendly spaces, conduct family tracing and re-unification, provide training and awareness at schools and host communities.

"Over 1 million people die due to Malaria and many other tropical diseases every year.”
